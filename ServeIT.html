Serve IT Summer Intern

Project Background

This was my first intern through Indiana University with Serve IT, it's a summer internship that's mainly for training and familiarization with different tools and the process of UX design. This project is made up of a team of five and we are in charge of redesigning a food pantry website.

Roles & Tools

  • Roles: Research, Communication, Web Design
  • Team: Yes
  • Tools: Trello, Slack, Notion, Figma, WordPress, LinkedIn, Element Pro, Adobe Illustrator, Google Doc
  • Duration: 12 week (Summer Term)

Overview

  • SiteMaps
  • Research & Document
  • Style Guides
  • Wireframes
  • Mobile Responsiveness
  • Final
  • Summary

SiteMaps

To start out our team wanted see a preview of what we neeed to do about this website where to start our process. To do that, we decided to use Figma to create a Sitemap. To clearly distinguish each individual's role, we also color coded the section we needs to work on.

Research & Document

After figuring out what we need to do we met with our client and we also drafted a google doc to document everything we might need to know about the website to redesign it. We took in information from each page on the original website and answered some questions about the mission, purpose, social media communication, operation, and any outside resources that may affect the functionality and layout of the website.

Style Guides

Next, our team decided to put together a style guide. We decide on the color, fonts, and the general layout of the site. There was also additional information to read and watch about how to design and create a style guide and why it was necessary for our team.

⭐ While I was working and reading information about style guides, It didn't came up to me that website accessibility has a lot to do with color contrast and font styles. Which I would like to keep that in mind from now on.

Wireframes

For Wireframes we did Low-Fidelity and Medium-Fidelity wireframing. Wireframes helps our team to see the connection better between informations on the site and how it should or can look once it's set.

Mobile Responsiveness

Besides wireframing, we were also trained to learn about mobile responsiveness. This is an important step to give users a better user experience with websites. It's asking us to pay attention to different screen sizes and adjust our website layout for easier access for the users and reduce frustration.

Final

During redesigning process we went back to the website and recheck things that might be easily forgotten, such as image alt text. Compare to the original website, we did a lot of changes including menu links, color scheme, font styles, and layout etc.

Summary

During this internship class with Serve IT, I learned a lot of useful skills and familiarized myself with different tools that I didn't before. I also learned more about cooperation and have a better understanding of user experience and web design. I appreciate this opportunity that was given to me and will remember what I learned.
